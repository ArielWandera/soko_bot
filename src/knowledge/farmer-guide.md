# Soko Farmer Guide

## Getting started as a farmer
Sign up at /auth/sign-up and select the "Farmer" role. You'll be asked to set your district and specialties (the crops you grow). After sign-up, complete your profile with your phone number so buyers can confirm orders.

## Creating a listing
Go to the Sell page (/sell). Fill in:
- **Product name** — be specific (e.g. "Maize Grain" not just "Maize")
- **Category** — vegetables, grains, fruits, cash_crops, dairy, herbs, livestock, or other
- **Description** — condition, harvest date, how it's stored, minimum order
- **Price per unit** — you can set your own or use the AI price suggestion based on current market data
- **Available quantity and minimum order**
- **District and village**
- **Photos** — at least one photo is required before you can publish

Once saved, the listing is in **draft**. Upload photos then click **Publish** to make it live.

## Pricing your produce
Use the AI price suggestion as a starting point — it's based on recent market prices across Uganda. You're free to price above or below it. Listings with competitive prices and good photos get more views.

## Managing orders
When a buyer places an order you'll get a notification. Go to your profile to see incoming orders. You must **confirm or reject within 24 hours**:
- **Confirm** — you have the stock and can fulfill the order
- **Reject** — you can't fulfill it (buyer is refunded automatically)

After confirming, mark the order **In Transit** when you dispatch, and **Delivered** when the buyer receives it.

## Getting paid
Payment is via MTN or Airtel Money through PesaPal. The buyer pays at checkout. Funds are held until you confirm the order. Once the order is completed, your payout is processed — check your registered Mobile Money number.

## Managing your listings
From your profile you can:
- Edit price, quantity, or description at any time
- Archive a listing (removes it from marketplace without deleting)
- Re-activate an archived listing

## Tips for more sales
- Upload clear, well-lit photos — listings with photos get significantly more views
- Keep your available quantity updated — buyers lose trust if they order and you don't have stock
- Respond to messages and orders quickly — fast response rate is factored into your recommendation score
- Maintain good ratings — buyers rate you after each order; fulfillment rate and ratings affect how often you appear in recommendations

## Ratings and reviews
After an order is completed, the buyer can leave a star rating (1–5) and a comment. Your average rating appears on your listings and affects your recommendation score. You cannot respond to reviews directly but consistent quality will improve your score over time.

## USSD access
If you don't have a smartphone, you can check current market prices via USSD. Ask the Soko team for the USSD shortcode for your network.

## What happens if a buyer disputes an order?
If a buyer reports an issue (wrong produce, short quantity, etc.) the order goes into dispute. Soko reviews the case. Maintain honest listings and accurate quantities to avoid disputes — repeated disputes affect your account standing.
